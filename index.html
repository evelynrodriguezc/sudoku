What is Sudoku?

Sudoku is a captivating numerical puzzle that challenges your brain and sharpens memory.

Its roots trace back to the 18th century in Switzerland, where mathematician Leonhard Euler developed the Latin Square concept. However, it transformed into the Sudoku we know today in the 20th century, thanks to Howard Garns, an American architect who published his version in Dell Magazines in 1979.

Sudoku gained local popularity quickly, but its global phenomenon began in Japan. First published in 1984 by Nikoli in their magazine, it was called Suuji wa dokushin ni kaguru (The numbers must remain single), later shortened to Suuji (数) and Doku (独). Nikoli introduced two key rules that set the stage for modern Sudoku: the initial numbers must form a visually appealing pattern, and no more than 32 of the 81 numbers can be given upfront, making each puzzle a thrilling challenge.
